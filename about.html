About

Product Manager working at the intersection of security and AI.

I'm a product manager with around five years of experience building and launching security products. My work spans the full arc of a product: taking new ideas from 0 to 1, standing up go-to-market and partner motions, running private previews, and creating the enablement that turns a launch into real adoption.

I've launched a new cloud-security product category across multiple clouds, built customer-validation engines for Zero Trust access, authored field-defining guidance for a major security-operations platform transition, and — most recently — worked at the frontier of AI-native security: making a SIEM consumable by AI agents through open protocols, and incubating a multi-model AI vulnerability scanner in private preview.

I also care about how teams work. I founded and led a v-team to design a hypothesis-driven “Gemba Walk” methodology that turns anecdotal partner feedback into repeatable engineering signal — because good process compounds.

How I work

  • Customer-obsessed. The best product decisions come from watching how people actually deploy and operate what you build.
  • Comfortable in ambiguity. I like the earliest stage — no category, no motion, no playbook — and building the path.
  • Bridge-builder. Translating between engineering, customers, and partners is where a lot of value is created (or lost).
  • Story + substance. Clear narrative and real technical depth aren't a trade-off; the best enablement has both.
