The problem

Following an acquisition, the company held a powerful new capability for managing cloud-permissions risk — but no established category, no go-to-market motion, and limited field understanding of why multi-cloud entitlement management mattered. Someone had to define the narrative, build the partner ecosystem, and prove the value with real customers across Azure, AWS, and GCP. That was my mandate.

What I drove

  • Owned the full go-to-market, deployment, and renewal motion for a brand-new CIEM offering spanning all three major clouds.
  • Built a 200+ partner launch ecosystem, qualifying 25 partners into co-sell, assessment, and managed-service motions.
  • Served as security PM on a flagship 100K+ user enterprise deployment, driving onboarding, adoption, and reference creation.
  • Turned field and customer friction into prioritized product feedback on reporting, remediation, APIs, onboarding, and licensing.

The impact

  • Helped stand up an entirely new product category and drove first-year adoption across Azure, AWS, and GCP.
  • Contributed to 25+ enterprise wins — an estimated $5M+ in influenced annual revenue, with individual deals ranging from ~$250K to ~$3M per year.
  • The flagship deployment surfaced 500+ inactive applications and enabled least-privilege, permissions-on-demand access — turning entitlement sprawl into a board-ready risk-reduction story.
  • Left behind a repeatable, partner-led motion that kept generating pipeline well beyond the initial launch.

What it showed

End-to-end product ownership: taking an unproven capability to market, building the ecosystem around it, and proving measurable business value through a real, large-scale customer deployment.
