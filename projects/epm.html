Launching a new CIEM product category

Took a newly acquired technology and stood up a Cloud Infrastructure Entitlement Management (CIEM) offering — from go-to-market to flagship deployment — across the three major clouds.

The problem

Following an acquisition, there was a powerful new capability for managing cloud permissions risk — but no established category, no go-to-market motion, and limited understanding in the field of why multi-cloud entitlement management mattered. Customers and partners needed help understanding the problem, positioning the product, and operationalizing least-privilege access across Azure, AWS, and GCP.

What I drove

  • Owned the go-to-market, deployment, and renewal motion for a brand-new CIEM offering across all three major clouds.
  • Built a launch partner ecosystem — 200+ partners engaged and dozens qualified into co-sell, assessment, and managed-service motions.
  • Led a flagship enterprise deployment at scale (100K+ users) as the assigned security PM, driving onboarding, adoption, and reference creation.
  • Translated field and customer friction into product feedback on reporting, remediation, APIs, onboarding, and licensing.

The impact

  • Helped establish a new product category from scratch and drove adoption across Azure, AWS, and GCP.
  • The flagship deployment surfaced 500+ inactive applications and enabled least-privilege / permissions-on-demand access — turning entitlement sprawl into a measurable risk-reduction story.
  • Contributed to 25+ enterprise wins and a repeatable partner-led motion that outlived the initial launch.

What it showed

This was end-to-end product ownership: taking an unproven capability to market, building the ecosystem around it, and proving value through a real, large-scale customer deployment.
