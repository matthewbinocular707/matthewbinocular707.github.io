Founding a “Gemba Walk” method for partners

Founded and led a cross-functional v-team to design a structured, hypothesis-driven “Gemba Walk” methodology — turning anecdotal partner feedback into a repeatable engineering signal.

The problem

Partner feedback about how security products were actually deployed and operated was mostly anecdotal — hard to act on, and easy to lose. There was no repeatable, disciplined way to observe real-world partner operations, uncover the root causes of friction, and convert those observations into signals engineering could prioritize.

What I led

  • Founded and led the v-team of partner-focused PMs as the project lead / global champion.
  • Authored the business requirements and designed the methodology — borrowing the “Gemba Walk” idea from continuous improvement: go to where the work happens and observe.
  • Built the operational toolkit — playbook, hypothesis library, pre-read templates, trackers, and a consistent insight-capture process.
  • Ran a 5-week pilot (Wave 1) across multiple security product areas and partner types, then socialized it as a repeatable continuous-improvement practice.

The impact

  • Replaced ad-hoc feedback with a hypothesis-driven, repeatable method that produces structured, prioritizable engineering signals.
  • Presented the practice to a ~40-person partner-PM organization for scaled adoption.
  • Created reusable assets that let others run the method without starting from scratch.

What it showed

Leadership and systems thinking: seeing a gap in how a whole org learns from partners, then designing — and getting adoption for — a durable process to close it.
