The problem

Organizations struggle to find complex vulnerabilities in large codebases with traditional, single-model scanning — and drown in false positives when they try. The opportunity: a next-generation system using a multi-model architecture that orchestrates 100+ specialized agents to find, validate, debate, and prove vulnerabilities end to end — surfacing previously unknown issues while cutting false positives through cross-model verification.

What I drove

  • Owned private-preview incubation and design-partner execution — onboarding, office hours, standups, and deployment motions.
  • Built the go-to-customer field motion from scratch: qualification, tenant onboarding and provisioning, readiness, and progression from interest to active use.
  • Ran structured feedback loops back to engineering and helped account teams navigate preview readiness and onboarding requirements.

The impact

  • Operationalized how an early-stage AI security capability reached design partners — the connective tissue between a research breakthrough and real-world adoption.
  • Established repeatable onboarding and feedback loops that let engineering learn quickly and sharpen accuracy during preview.
  • Helped position the capability against a problem — undiscovered software vulnerabilities — that costs enterprises billions annually.

What it showed

Comfort operating at the earliest, most ambiguous stage of a product — where the job is to build the go-to-customer motion from scratch and create the feedback loops that make an emerging AI capability adoptable.
