Incubating a multi-model AI vulnerability scanner

Led private-preview incubation and field readiness for a next-generation AI security scanner that orchestrates 100+ specialized agents to find, validate, and prove software vulnerabilities.

  • Agentic AI

  • Private preview
  • Customer onboarding

The problem

Organizations increasingly struggle to find complex vulnerabilities in large codebases using traditional, single-model scanning. The opportunity: a next-generation, AI-powered system that uses a multi-model architecture instead of a single model — orchestrating 100+ specialized agents to find, validate, debate, and prove vulnerabilities end to end, discovering previously unknown issues while reducing false positives through cross-model verification.

What I drove

  • Owned private-preview incubation and design-partner execution — onboarding, office hours, standups, and deployment motions.
  • Built the field motion: preview-customer qualification, tenant onboarding and provisioning, readiness, and progression from interest to active use.
  • Ran structured feedback collection back to engineering, and helped account teams navigate preview readiness and onboarding requirements.

The impact

  • Helped operationalize how an early-stage AI security capability reached customers and partners — the connective tissue between a research breakthrough and real-world adoption.
  • Established repeatable onboarding and feedback loops that let engineering learn quickly from preview design partners.

What it showed

Comfort operating at the earliest, most ambiguous stage of a product — where the job is to build the go-to-customer motion from scratch and create the feedback loops that make an emerging AI capability adoptable.
