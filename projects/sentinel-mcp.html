Making a SIEM AI-native with MCP

Drove product storytelling and preview enablement for an AI-native security capability — letting external AI agents and copilots work directly with a SIEM through the open Model Context Protocol.

  • AI agents
  • Model Context Protocol
  • Security Copilot
  • Positioning
  • Preview enablement

The problem

Before standardized protocols, every AI system needed a custom integration to every security tool and data source. Those integrations were fragmented, fragile, inconsistent, and slow to scale — security teams had to build and maintain separate connections for each platform. The result: AI-assisted security work was hard to operationalize.

What I drove

  • Created the product narrative and positioning for a SIEM's Model Context Protocol (MCP) capability — framing it as a way to make the platform consumable by any compatible AI client (copilots, coding assistants, and agents).
  • Authored the flagship positioning and preview decks and announcement/enablement content demonstrating agent-driven investigations against the SIEM.
  • Ran partner preview engagement and feedback loops, and led adoption conversations with customers and managed-security providers.
  • Evangelized MCP and agentic security workflows across engineering, customer-experience, and partner teams.

The impact

  • Helped translate a low-level protocol into a practical security-operations capability — one standard instead of many brittle integrations.
  • Produced some of the earliest enablement content connecting the SIEM to third-party AI clients, seeding partner and customer interest during preview.
  • Positioned the platform as AI-native at a moment when agentic tooling was becoming central to security operations.

What it showed

Product sense at the frontier: spotting where an emerging open standard changes what's possible, then doing the storytelling and enablement to make it real for customers and partners.
