The problem

A major security operations platform was moving its SIEM experience into a unified XDR portal — converging SIEM, XDR, AI, automation, and data-lake capabilities. For enterprise SOCs and managed-security providers, the change raised hard questions about architecture, permissions, workflows, and APIs — and many in the field saw only disruption and risk, not the modernization opportunity underneath it.

What I drove

  • Authored the flagship technical migration guide — covering transition planning, roles and permissions, feature and API changes, and operational impact.
  • Created customer- and partner-perspective playbooks that explained the strategic rationale, business impact, and value proposition of unified security operations.
  • Wrote thought-leadership content positioning the change as a modernization journey — unified SIEM + XDR, AI copilot, data lake, and attack disruption — rather than a UI migration.
  • Developed deep-dives on specific workflow changes (incident and case management) and a readiness/adoption playbook.

The impact

  • Became the reusable source of truth for the transition, adapted across enterprise and MSSP engagements representing multimillion-dollar modernization opportunities.
  • Shifted partner conversations from "why is the portal changing?" to "how do we modernize the SOC?" — unlocking larger, strategic deals.
  • Reduced ambiguity and de-risked migrations for customers and partners navigating a complex platform change.

What it showed

Turning a complex platform change into a clear, business-oriented narrative — and producing enablement content durable and credible enough to be reused across the field.
