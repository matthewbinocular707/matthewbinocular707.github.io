Security Operations · Enablement

Guiding a unified SecOps platform transition

Created the field's go-to guidance for a major security operations transition — a SIEM moving into a unified XDR portal — and reframed a portal change into a SOC-modernization opportunity.

  • SIEM + XDR
  • Technical writing
  • Partner enablement
  • Migration strategy
  • Thought leadership

The problem

A major security operations platform was transitioning its SIEM experience into a unified XDR portal — bringing together SIEM, XDR, AI, automation, and data-lake capabilities. The move raised real operational, architectural, permissions, workflow, and API questions for enterprise SOCs and managed-security providers. Customers needed practical guidance on what was changing, why it mattered, how to migrate, and how to get value beyond a simple portal move.

What I drove

  • Authored the flagship technical migration guide — covering transition planning, roles and permissions, feature and API changes, and operational impact.
  • Created customer- and partner-perspective playbooks that explained the strategic rationale, business impact, and value proposition of unified security operations.
  • Wrote thought-leadership content positioning the change as a modernization journey — unified SIEM + XDR, AI copilot, data lake, and attack disruption — rather than a UI migration.
  • Developed deep-dives on specific workflow changes (incident and case management) and a readiness/adoption playbook.

The impact

  • My guidance became a reusable field asset, adapted for many different enterprise and managed-security-provider engagements.
  • It shifted partner conversations from "why is the portal changing?" to "how do we modernize the SOC?"
  • Established a technical source of truth that reduced ambiguity for customers and partners navigating the transition.

What it showed

Turning a complex platform change into a clear, business-oriented narrative — and producing enablement content durable and credible enough to be reused across the field.
