Scaling a Security Service Edge preview

Built the customer-validation engine for a Security Service Edge (Zero Trust network access) suite — turning early-access feedback into product direction and adoption.

  • Zero Trust
  • Private preview
  • Customer validation
  • Field enablement
  • Roadmap influence
  • 85+Engineering work items driven
  • 140Resellers enabled
  • 3Launch customer references

The problem

A new Security Service Edge suite was coming to market to help customers move off legacy VPNs toward identity-centric, Zero Trust access. The product team needed real-world feedback, design partners and preview customers, validation of roadmap priorities, customer references for launch, and field and partner readiness.

What I drove

  • Stood up a customer feedback and validation engine — channelling field and customer insight into 85+ engineering work items across the product's access capabilities.
  • Scaled the private preview: nominated 18 customers, onboarded selected design partners, and expanded feedback channels with dozens more.
  • Ran partner and field readiness reaching 140 resellers, and authored L200/L300 customer enablement decks used broadly in the field.
  • Advocated for customer pain points in roadmap and prioritization discussions, and surfaced packaging/licensing opportunities.

The impact

  • Turned scattered feedback into a structured product-input pipeline that engineering could act on.
  • Secured 3 customer references that were featured during the product's public-preview launch at a flagship event.
  • Accelerated field readiness so partners could position and sell the new capability with confidence.

What it showed

Bridging engineering and customers: building the connective tissue — previews, references, enablement — that a launch depends on, and making the customer voice a first-class input to the roadmap.
