The problem

A new Security Service Edge suite was coming to market to help customers move off legacy VPNs toward identity-centric, Zero Trust access. To land a credible launch, the product team needed real-world feedback, design partners, validated roadmap priorities, reference customers, and a field and partner channel ready to position and sell it — none of which existed yet.

What I drove

  • Stood up a customer feedback and validation engine, channelling field and customer insight into 85+ engineering work items across the product's access capabilities.
  • Scaled the private preview — nominated 18 customers, onboarded selected design partners, and expanded feedback channels with dozens more.
  • Ran partner and field readiness across a 140-reseller channel and authored the L200/L300 customer enablement decks used broadly in the field.
  • Championed customer pain points in roadmap and prioritization reviews, and surfaced packaging and licensing opportunities.

The impact

  • Turned scattered feedback into a structured product-input pipeline engineering could act on — directly shaping 85+ work items.
  • Secured 3 marquee customer references featured during the product's public-preview launch at a flagship event.
  • Readied a 140-reseller channel, accelerating time-to-sell for a Zero Trust product line representing a multimillion-dollar market opportunity.

What it showed

Bridging engineering and customers: building the connective tissue — previews, references, enablement — that every launch depends on, and making the customer voice a first-class input to the roadmap.
